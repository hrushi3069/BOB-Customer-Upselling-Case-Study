# BOB Customer Upselling Case Study

This project is a business analytics case study focused on customer segmentation and upselling strategy in an e-commerce environment. It follows the CRISP-DM methodology to analyse customer behaviour and identify opportunities for increasing revenue through targeted marketing.

---

## Project Objective

The main objectives of this case study are:

- Understand customer purchasing behaviour
- Segment customers based on transactional data
- Identify high-value customers for upselling
- Support data-driven marketing decisions
- Improve customer lifetime value (CLV)

---

## Methodology (CRISP-DM)

This project follows the CRISP-DM framework:

### 1. Business Understanding
Define upselling opportunities and customer segmentation goals.

### 2. Data Understanding
Explore customer transaction data, spending patterns, and frequency of purchases.

### 3. Data Preparation
- Data cleaning and missing value handling
- Feature engineering (RFM analysis)
- Aggregation of customer-level metrics

### 4. Modelling
- Customer segmentation using clustering techniques (e.g., K-Means)
- Scoring customers based on value and engagement

### 5. Evaluation
- Cluster quality assessment
- Business interpretation of segments

### 6. Deployment
- Upselling strategy recommendations based on segments

---

## Techniques Used

- RFM Analysis (Recency, Frequency, Monetary Value)
- K-Means Clustering
- Data preprocessing and feature engineering
- Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Business segmentation logic

---

## Key Insights

- A small percentage of customers contribute to most revenue
- High-frequency customers respond best to upselling
- Low-engagement customers require retention strategies
- Customer segmentation improves targeting efficiency

---

## Business Recommendations

- Focus upselling on high-value customer segments
- Offer personalised discounts to mid-value customers
- Re-engage inactive customers with targeted campaigns
- Use customer segmentation to optimise marketing budget
